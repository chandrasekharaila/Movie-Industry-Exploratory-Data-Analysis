# Movie Industry Exploratory Data Analysis

## Overview

This project performs Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on a movie industry dataset to understand the factors that influence movie success and box office performance.

The analysis focuses on movie budgets, ratings, genres, actors, directors, countries, and revenue to identify patterns and generate business insights.

---

## Business Objectives

The analysis aims to answer the following questions:

- Which actors appear in the most movies?
- Which actors generate the highest box office revenue?
- Which directors have the highest average IMDb ratings?
- Which genres perform best financially?
- Does a larger budget lead to higher revenue?
- Do highly rated movies earn more at the box office?
- Which factors are most strongly related to movie success?

---

## Dataset Features

The dataset contains information about movies, including:

- Movie Title
- Release Date
- Genre
- Country
- Lead Actor
- Director
- Budget (USD)
- US Box Office Revenue
- Global Box Office Revenue
- IMDb Rating
- Rotten Tomatoes Score

## Key Findings

- Movies with larger budgets generally generate higher global box office revenue.
- Revenue is concentrated among a small number of blockbuster films.
- Certain genres consistently outperform others financially.
- Actor and director influence appears to impact movie performance.
- Higher-rated movies tend to generate better box office results.
- Financial variables show strong positive relationships with one another.

---

## Business Recommendations

- Focus investments on historically successful genres.
- Consider actor and director track records during project planning.
- Use ratings as one factor when evaluating movie performance.
- Balance production budgets with expected returns.
- Analyze successful movies to identify characteristics that drive revenue.
